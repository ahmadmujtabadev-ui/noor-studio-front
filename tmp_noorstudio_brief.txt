NoorStudio — Alpha Review — Consolidated Dev Brief 
Executive summary 
The dev team has done outstanding work pulling NoorStudio from MVP to a coherent end-to-end 
product. The full creation journey — from Universe through Knowledge Base, Character, Story, Structure, 
Illustrations, Cover, and Publish — is functional, the information architecture is thoughtful, and the 
cultural sensitivity embedded in the product (Islamic values library, du'as, modesty rules, tasbih-in-hand 
accessories) shows real care for the audience. NoorStudio is materially ahead of typical alpha-stage AI 
tools in this category. 
The work required now is tightening. This review consolidates founder feedback and a full-flow audit into 
a single set of tickets. The issues fall into a handful of clear buckets: 
•​
Positioning — Muslim-first, universally welcoming — NoorStudio is proudly Muslim in heritage 
and acquisition identity, universal in values and retention. Muslim families don't only want 
Islamic content — they want the full breadth of children's stories (adventure, animals, bedtime, 
fantasy, friendship) with values they trust. The product should serve that full breadth as a 
first-class experience, with Islamic-Forward content as the flagship flavour and Universal 
Wholesome content as an equal first-class option. 80/20 in stage 1: 80% Muslim-first in 
marketing voice, 20% welcoming open door for non-Muslim values-aligned families. Evolution is 
data-driven and deliberately undefined. 
•​
Three product tiers, two distinct users — Creator tier for parent-and-kid book-making (Amina 
persona — the kid becomes a published author, viral mum-network referral engine). Studio tier 
for entrepreneur mums monetising through KDP (Sara persona — series creation, deep KB, 
high-LTV). Plus existing advanced tier. Kid use is welcomed within the current UX with parental 
guidance and video training — no separate kid-mode in this release. 
•​
Onboarding and flow clarity — users currently cannot tell what a Universe is, what order to 
create things in, or how far they've progressed end-to-end. A guided explainer, a fixed 
creation-order, and a persistent master progress indicator would close this almost entirely. 
•​
The Knowledge Base concept is under-communicated — the KB is the single most important 
feature in the product — it is the DNA that preserves a series across many books. But in the 
current UI it is presented as a set of tabs with fields, not as the continuity engine it actually is. 
Reframing it as "Book DNA" at every touchpoint, adding a KB Strength score, and making its 
concrete impact visible on Book Foundation are the three changes that transform the product 
from an AI story generator into a series-grade publishing platform. 
•​
Broken or half-working features — character pose library renders empty after generation, 
Arabic text overlays on illustrations are mangled, and back-cover generation is unreliable. These 
are alpha-grade but ship-blocking for a paid product. 
•​
Copyright and brand exposure — "Pixar 3D" and "Ghibli" as style labels, and several cover 
templates that visually evoke copyrighted properties, create real legal risk that should be 
resolved before any paid launch. 
•​
Audience-fit wording — "Chubby" as a child body type, explicit weight-in-kg field, 
"Other/Creature" gender lumping, and a few skin-tone labels will create discomfort with the 
Muslim family audience who are the core customer. 
•​
Cognitive load on long forms — Visual DNA and several KB sections ask for 20–30+ decisions on 
a single scroll with no preview and no conditional collapsing. Templates exist but are 
under-surfaced.

A. Legal, brand, and IP 
T-01 
P0 
Art Style picker · Visual DNA · Edit Universe 
Rename "Pixar 3D" and "Ghibli" style labels 
Area: Copyright / Brand risk    Source: Flow audit 
Issue: "Pixar 3D" and "Ghibli" are registered studio names. Using them as UI labels on a commercial AI 
generation product creates direct brand-infringement exposure and takedown risk. This risk is elevated 
for an Islamic children's platform where trust is the core value proposition. 
Recommendation: Rename to generic descriptors: "Pixar 3D" → "3D Rendered" or "Stylized 3D". 
"Ghibli" → "Hand-Painted Anime" or "Watercolor Anime". Audit any downstream prompt templates that 
pass these names to the image model and replace with the same generic descriptors. 
Acceptance criteria: 
•​
No UI label references "Pixar," "Ghibli," "Disney," or any other studio/brand name anywhere in 
the product 
•​
Backend prompt templates contain no studio/brand names when generating images 
•​
Style previews updated with original thumbnails that do not evoke the renamed studios 
 
T-02 
P0 
Knowledge Base → Cover Design 
Audit Cover Style Template gallery for IP-adjacent designs 
Area: Cover template gallery / IP risk    Source: Flow audit 
Issue: Several cover templates in the gallery visually evoke specific, identifiable commercial children's 
book series and films — the dragon-flame cover, the moon-over-city cover, and the watercolor floral 
cover in particular sit close to recognisable IP. This creates exposure both at the platform level and for 
users generating derivative covers. 
Recommendation: Design or commission 10 original cover templates that cover the same stylistic range 
(adventurous, cinematic, heritage, ornate, minimal, watercolour, cosmic, storybook, bold typographic, 
nature) without leaning on any specific commercial reference. Regenerate template preview art cleanly. 
Acceptance criteria: 
•​
Every cover template is visually original with no direct evocation of identifiable commercial 
properties 
•​
Template names are generic style descriptors, not character or property names 
•​
Backend prompt seeds behind each template are reviewed for prompt-level IP leakage

B. Audience fit, wording, and sensitivity 
T-03 
P0 
Visual DNA (Create Character) · Universe settings 
Add master "Family Values Mode" toggle with Shariah-Compliant as named preset 
Area: Values guardrails / trust signal    Source: Flow audit + founder positioning reframe    Related: T-61, T-62 
Issue: Families using NoorStudio have values-driven content concerns (modesty, no alcohol, no romance 
for young readers, no violence, faith framing preferences) and currently have to verify multiple individual 
settings to trust the output. This cognitive load hits hardest with the core Muslim audience, but also 
applies to every values-aligned family the product welcomes — the universal wholesome segment, 
conservative Christian families, Jewish families, secular values-driven families. A single master control is 
needed. Given the product's positioning (Muslim-proud, universal-welcome, 80/20), the mechanism 
should be tradition-neutral in name but ship with Shariah-Compliant as the flagship named preset so 
Muslim users immediately see the term they expect. 
Recommendation: Add a prominent master toggle above the fold on Visual DNA (and in Universe 
settings) labelled "Family Values Mode." Inside it, a preset selector offers named configurations: (1) 
Shariah-Compliant — default for Islamic-Forward Universes, sets Long Sleeves ON, Loose Clothing ON, 
hijab ON for girls aged 9+, no form-fitting, covers knees, no alcohol references, no romance, Arabic 
phrases natural; (2) Wholesome Universal — default for Universal Wholesome Universes, sets no 
alcohol, no romance for young readers, age-appropriate content, modesty off by default but 
configurable; (3) Custom — user configures each guardrail individually. Other faith presets 
(Christian-Conservative, Jewish-Orthodox-friendly, etc.) appear as "Coming soon" roadmap placeholders 
per the explicit de-scope. The Shariah-Compliant preset must feel first-class and polished — it is the 
flagship. For non-Islamic-Forward Universes, Family Values Mode still exists and is still useful; it simply 
defaults to Wholesome Universal. Crucially: the term "Shariah-Compliant" is not hidden or softened — it 
is moved from master-toggle-name to preset-name where it remains prominent for Muslim users. 
Acceptance criteria: 
•​
Master toggle labelled "Family Values Mode" visible above the fold on Visual DNA and in 
Universe settings 
•​
Preset selector offers Shariah-Compliant, Wholesome Universal, and Custom 
•​
Other faith presets visible as "Coming soon" roadmap placeholders, not built in v1 
•​
Default preset is determined by the Universe's flavour (Islamic-Forward → Shariah-Compliant, 
Universal Wholesome → Wholesome Universal) 
•​
When a preset is selected, relevant fields are locked with a lock icon and tooltip e.g. "Locked by 
Family Values Mode (Shariah-Compliant preset)" 
•​
Switching to Custom unlocks all fields for granular configuration 
•​
Switching preset or turning the mode off requires a confirmation dialog explaining what will 
change 
•​
State is persisted per-Universe and per-Character 
•​
The term "Shariah-Compliant" remains visible and prominent for Muslim users as the flagship 
preset name

Audit and rebalance visual assets for universal-first breadth 
Area: Visual asset rebalance    Source: Flow audit / positioning    Related: T-21, T-54 
Issue: Almost every preview image, template thumbnail, empty-state illustration, and onboarding visual 
in the product today is overtly Islamic — hijabis, minarets, crescents, mosque interiors, Andalusian 
arches. This signals to every new user that NoorStudio makes Islamic books only. Muslim families who 
want to write a butterfly story see this and assume the tool is wrong for their current need. Non-Muslim

invisible because the visual language doesn't show it. 
Recommendation: Full visual asset audit. For every template preview, empty state, marketing image, 
and illustrative asset, ensure the library reflects the product's actual breadth. Approximate target mix: 
40% Muslim-coded imagery (hijabis, Islamic settings, Arabic calligraphy), 40% universal imagery (diverse 
kids, animals, adventure scenes, everyday family life, nature, fantasy), 20% culturally-diverse imagery 
(Muslim and non-Muslim kids of different ethnicities together, travel, heritage sites of various traditions). 
Muslim imagery stays strong and high-quality — this is a rebalance, not a dilution. Current Islamic assets 
are retained as the Islamic-Forward flavour's visual library. 
Acceptance criteria: 
•​
Every template gallery (character, KB, cover) has visual breadth across Muslim, universal, and 
diverse imagery 
•​
Empty-state illustrations show a mix of story types, not only Islamic scenes 
•​
First-run onboarding visuals reflect the product's actual breadth 
•​
Dashboard carousels and marketing-style preview tiles rebalanced to approximately 40/40/20 
split 
•​
Muslim imagery remains best-in-class — highest-quality assets, most polished

G. Knowledge Base — concept and framing 
The core issue with KB today 
The Knowledge Base is the single most important feature in NoorStudio, and also the least 
well-explained. It is what makes the product different from a generic AI story generator. But in the 
current UI, the KB is presented as a set of four tabs with fields to fill in. It looks like a preferences panel. It 
is not a preferences panel. 
The real concept: KB is Book DNA. 
Page 25 


===== PAGE 26 =====

NoorStudio — Alpha Review — Consolidated Dev Brief 
The KB is the library of rules, resources, tone of voice, visual style, vocabulary, values, and world-facts 
that preserve a series' DNA across many books. A Universe is the container. The KB is the continuity 
engine that keeps book 20 feeling like it belongs to book 1. Without a well-populated KB, stories and 
illustrations drift — characters talk differently in each book, the world looks different each time, the 
Islamic values feel bolted on instead of woven in. With a well-populated KB, every generated asset across 
every book in the series pulls from the same DNA. 
Relationship to Universe: the KB governs a Universe, and a Universe has a KB. One-to-one conceptually. 
Whether the implementation stores KBs as children of Universes or as shareable libraries referenced by 
multiple Universes is an implementation decision — either works. What matters is that the user 
understands: the KB is the DNA that survives across books in the same series. 
The following tickets address this conceptual gap first, then go into the specific content-depth 
improvements for each KB tab.

Reframe the Knowledge Base as "Book DNA" at every user touchpoint

Reference Library — parked concept, future phase 
During review, the question arose whether the Knowledge Base should eventually become a real 
reference library — a RAG-backed corpus of sourced Islamic knowledge (Seerah, Fiqh, Hadith, Muslim 
History, Qur'anic stories, Sahabah biographies, Islamic geography and architecture, Golden Age sciences). 
Every generation call would ground itself in vetted, scholar-reviewed sources. Every output would carry 
provenance: "This story references the Mezquita of Cordoba [source], the life of Ibn Rushd [source], and 
the du'a upon entering a masjid [Sunan Abu Dawud, sahih]." 
This is directionally exciting for three reasons. First, it is the only feature that would make NoorStudio 
categorically different from every other AI children's book tool — competitors can copy UX and 
templates, but not scholarly-vetted corpora. Second, it unlocks the institutional tier (Islamic schools, 
madrasas) where willingness-to-pay is 5–10x the consumer tier. Third, the same architecture could 
eventually support non-religious reference libraries (Scouts, child development psychology, STEM, 
Christian homeschool, etc.) — a much broader moat. 
It is parked for v1 because: (a) the current audience (Amina and Sara personas) does not yet need it — 
most children's books they'll make are fiction and don't require historical grounding, (b) the build cost is 
significant (3–6 months engineering plus ongoing scholarly review and content curation), (c) it distracts 
from shipping Creator and Studio tiers to PMF, (d) the strongest signal for when to build it will come from 
Studio-tier users asking for it, not from our internal excitement. 
Design principle for v1: ensure the KB data model can accommodate "linked reference corpora" as a field 
later, even though no corpora exist. Zero current cost, high option value. When the time comes to build 
it, the data model doesn't need refactoring. 
Trigger for revisiting: (i) 100+ paying Studio-tier users, (ii) at least 3 inbound inquiries from Islamic 
schools or madrasas, (iii) Creator and Studio tiers are at PMF with stable retention. 
Naima Robert distribution strategy 
The Studio tier (Sara persona) has a named, warm distribution channel: Naima Robert's book authoring 
academy for mothers (140K followers, established Muslim-mum author community, paid-course 
audience already primed on the book-writing-as-business value proposition). This is captured as T-63 in

Naima's academy is pre-qualified Sara — her students have already self-identified as wanting to

Three-tier architecture vision 
The product serves two distinct users through three tiers. Captured here as product vision for reference 
when making feature decisions: 
•​
Creator tier — Amina persona — parent-and-kid book-making. Low price point (impulse-level — 
user tested ~£12/month range). Wizard-style first-book flow. KB hidden behind smart defaults. 
Emotional hook: the kid becomes a published author. The Layla-gets-her-book-on-KDP moment. 
Viral referral engine — mums share in WhatsApp and Islamic-school groups. Top of funnel for the 
entire product. 
•​
Author tier — existing mid-tier — whatever the current mid-subscription is. Bridges Creator and 
Studio. Users who make more than a handful of books but aren't yet running a business. 
•​
Studio tier — Sara persona — entrepreneur mums monetising book series through KDP. Higher 
price point (business-investment-level — user tested ~£39–59/month range). Full KB and 
Universe management exposed. Series consistency tooling, KDP export optimisation, cover A/B 
testing, analytics. Reached primarily through Naima Robert channel (T-63). Revenue engine — 
higher LTV, lower churn, business-driven retention. 
Positioning per tier matters: Creator is emotional ("give your child the pride of being a published 
author"), Studio is commercial ("turn your storytelling into a real income stream"). The two messages 
should not be mixed in the same marketing funnel. 
Evolution criteria — when to revisit major decisions 
Several decisions in this brief are stage-appropriate but will need revisiting. Capturing the triggers here: 
•​
Non-Muslim faith packs — revisit when non-Muslim sign-ups exceed 15% of new users or when 
a specific community (e.g. Christian homeschool) reaches out as a cohort. 
•​
Reference Library — revisit at 100+ Studio-tier paying users, or 3+ inbound inquiries from Islamic 
schools/madrasas. 
•​
Kid-mode dedicated UI — revisit when user research shows kids aged 8–13 are active primary 
users (not just supervised co-creators). 
•​
B2B / institutional tier — revisit post Reference Library decision — they are tied together 
commercially. 
•​
80/20 audience balance — revisit quarterly based on actual user mix and retention signals.
